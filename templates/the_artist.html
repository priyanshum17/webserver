The Artist: Rick Astley

Richard Paul Astley (born 6 February 1966) is an English singer, songwriter, and radio personality. His career began in the 1980s, and he rose to fame with his 1987 hit single "Never Gonna Give You Up."

Early Life and Career

Astley was born in Newton-le-Willows, Lancashire, England. He began his musical career as a drummer in a local band before being discovered by music producer Pete Waterman. He signed with PWL Records in 1985.

Rise to Stardom

Under the guidance of Stock Aitken Waterman, Astley released "Never Gonna Give You Up," which propelled him to international fame. He followed this success with a string of other hits, including "Together Forever" and "Whenever You Need Somebody."

Hiatus and Comeback

In 1993, Astley retired from music to focus on his family. He made a comeback in the early 2000s and has since released new music and toured extensively, embracing his iconic status and the unexpected resurgence of "Never Gonna Give You Up" through the Rickrolling phenomenon.
